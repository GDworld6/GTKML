THE QUESTION WE OFTEN FIND OURSELVES ASKING

By Monalisa Liabunya

We all want to be free. We all want to be happy. We all want to feel in control of our minds. But the truth about the mind is this: sometimes, we feel out of control and detached from it. Not because we are afraid, but because parts of our subconscious rise into our conscious in ways we do not always understand.

It is easy to believe that you have everything figured out. That you know your demons. That you are in control of your emotions and your feelings.

But there is always that one moment that triggers a part of you you never knew existed. That moment can lead to alcohol or drug abuse. It can lead to self-sabotage. It can cause you to crash out, to shut down, to put your guard up. The list goes on. Then suddenly, you snap back to reality, and you have no idea why you reacted the way you did.

It is easy to ignore it and move on.

But how many of us actually sit down and ask ourselves: Why is this happening to me? Why am I like this? Why did I go through that? And if God exists, why did He allow it?

My favourite questions are these: Why did I go through that? And if God exists, why did He allow it?

I replay these questions like my favourite melodies. Not because I have the answers, but because they help me understand what it means to be human, and what it means to be an individual.

The more I grow, the more I realize that everything that has happened to me was meant for something. Some may say that is a sad way to look at life. But I believe it is one of the most powerful ways to see it.

Life has a strange way of working itself out. It mirrors back to you in ways you never imagined.

This is why one of my favourite teachings is when Jesus said, “Do not judge, or you too will be judged.” And, “Let the one who has never sinned cast the first stone.”

As a child, and even as someone still learning, I used to take these words lightly. I never understood their depth. Now I do. It is never wise to speak on what you do not understand, especially when it comes to someone’s life, their choices, or their way of living. You never truly know what options they have, what they are carrying, or what they are going through.

I know that is a hard truth to accept, especially when we consider how we were raised and the beliefs we have held onto.

If you are like me, someone raised in a Christian household and someone who believes in God, then you may understand this: everything happens for a reason. That reason may not make sense to us or to anyone else, but if God allowed it, then perhaps it serves a greater purpose for our good.

We just have to believe that God is for us, and that life, in its own way, is also for us.

The next time you find yourself asking, “Why me?” or “Why is God allowing this?” pause for a moment.

Look at everything you have overcome. Look at the times you felt lost, both in your conscious and subconscious, yet still found your way back to balance. Look at everything you have accomplished.

Then ask yourself: If I am not meant to be here, why am I still alive? Why is God, or the universe, allowing me to exist?

Is it because I have not yet fulfilled my purpose? And if so, what is that purpose? Am I worthy of being alive?

Do not let yourself believe that what is happening to you is simply unfair. Do not dwell on how people have treated you or perceived you. Instead, ask a different question: what is God, or the universe, trying to teach me?

And in that pursuit, do not forget to be grateful.

Be grateful for what you have. Be grateful for what you have been through. Be grateful for what you have lived and experienced.

The mistakes, the losses, the confusion, the pain. All of it.

Because without everything, you would not have anything.
